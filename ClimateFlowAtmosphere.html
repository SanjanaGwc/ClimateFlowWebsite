Global carbon emissions in 2018 hit an all-time record of 37.1 billion metric tons

The greenhouse effect is a term used to discuss global warming relating to the atmosphere. Greenhouse gases include water vapor, carbon dioxide, nitrous oxide, and methane. These gases trap energy from the sun; without them, heat would escape and the Earth would be much colder. The current problem is that human activities (such as burning fossil fuels) release much more carbon dioxide into our atmosphere. A large amount of greenhouse gases can cause Earth to trap more heat, and essentially, overheat the globe. The impact of this is not just higher temperatures; it can also lead to more intense storms, colder temperatures in northern areas, drier deserts, droughts or increased flooding.
